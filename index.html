Abstract

Understanding high-dimensional data distributions remains a core challenge in modern experimental physics and machine learning. This work presents the HD-Dataset, a meticulously curated collection of multi-modal observations designed to benchmark inference algorithms in noisy environments.

Our dataset spans across several experimental configurations, providing over 5TB of high-fidelity data points, each accompanied by precise metadata and ground-truth simulations.

Methodology

Our pipeline utilizes state-of-the-art sensors synchronized via a centralized temporal alignment system. Data is processed through a three-stage noise reduction filter before being voxelized into a 4D spatio-temporal grid.

We provide multiple versions of the data (Raw, Processed, and Compressed) to accommodate different research needs.
